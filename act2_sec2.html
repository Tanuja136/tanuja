The dog has been selectively bred over millennia for various behaviors,
sensory capabilities, and physical attributes. Dog breeds vary widely in shape, size, and color.
They perform many roles for humans, such as hunting, herding.

The dog is a classic example of a domestic animal that likely travelled a commensal pathway into domestication.[15][21] The questions of when and where dogs were first domesticated have taxed geneticists and archaeologists for decades.[16] Genetic studies suggest a domestication process commencing over 25,000 years ago, in one or several wolf populations in either Europe, the high Arctic, or eastern Asia
